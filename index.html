Brief Bio

I'm a Physics Ph.D. Student heading into my second year at UC Irvine where I work with Professor James Bullock. Our group utilizes large cosmolagical simulations to study the formation of galexies throughout time. We primarily focus our studies on the Local Group galaxies; i.e., the Milky Way, Andromeda (M31), and the small galaxies that surround them.

Research

My previous work as an undergraduate research student focussed on helping me develop the tools necessary to run numerical simulations (using the GIZMO code) and to analyze their output. Recently, as part of the Local Group here at UCI, I've started studying the hot gas content (T > 106 K) of Milky Way like galaxies using the LATTE and the ELVIS simulations. I've also recently started studying the metallicity gradients of dwarf galaxies using two suites of simulations developed by Andrew Graus and Alex Fitts.

Community Outreach

I've participated in outreach events planned by various organizations that work to encourage young students to pursue higher education both in and out of STEM fields. As a Latino student, I'm interested in partnering with graduate students of other underrepresented backgrounds and taking these outreach events to elementary and middle schools primarily populated by students of similar backgrounds. Doing this is important because young students need to see people like them flourishing in order to project themselves in those successful roles.
